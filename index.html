Case Study

Foundations of Effective Support: eLearning Module

Role: Learning Experience Designer · Format: Self-paced eLearning · Tools: Articulate Rise / Storyline, Canva

Project Overview

This project is a self-paced eLearning module designed to onboard new staff working in a high-contact, service-oriented environment. The goal was to build foundational skills in communication, empathy, and systems navigation so that new team members could confidently support clients from day one.

Objectives

  • Clarify key responsibilities and expectations for new staff in the first 30 days.
  • Introduce core communication and de-escalation strategies through realistic scenarios.
  • Provide interactive practice with branching situations and knowledge checks.
  • Offer reusable reference materials that can be revisited on the job.

Design Approach

I began with a performance-focused needs analysis to identify where new staff typically struggled. From there, I mapped those gaps to specific learning outcomes and designed a modular experience that blended microlearning, short scenario videos, reflection prompts, and low-stakes assessments.

  • Storyboards and scripts built around real learner pain points.
  • Scenario-based questions tied to real-world decisions, not just recall.
  • Simple navigation and clean visual hierarchy to reduce cognitive load.
  • Embedded job aids learners could download and reference later.

Outcomes & Feedback

Early feedback from learners and managers highlighted that the module made expectations feel concrete and “doable,” and shortened the amount of coaching time required after onboarding. Qualitative comments emphasized clarity, realism of examples, and ease of use.
